Incentives

Stack every solar incentive you qualify for

Federal, state and utility programs can cut your effective solar cost dramatically. Here's how they layer together.

Three Layers of Savings

How incentives stack

The magic of solar economics is combining incentives — each one compounding the next.

Federal Incentives

The 30% Residential Clean Energy Credit and commercial ITC + MACRS depreciation apply nationwide.

  • 30% federal tax credit
  • MACRS depreciation (commercial)
  • USDA REAP grants (agricultural)

State Incentives

Many states add their own credits, rebates and tax exemptions on top of the federal credit.

  • State tax credits (e.g. AZ 25%)
  • Sales & property-tax exemptions
  • State storage rebates (e.g. SGIP)

Utility Incentives

Local utilities often offer rebates, performance payments and favorable net-metering programs.

  • Utility cash rebates
  • Net metering / net billing
  • Performance-based incentives
