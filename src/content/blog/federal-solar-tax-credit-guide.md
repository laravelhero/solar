If you own your home and pay federal income tax, the **Residential Clean Energy Credit** is the most valuable solar incentive available to you. Here's how to make the most of it.

## What the credit is

The federal solar tax credit lets you claim **30% of the total cost** of your solar energy system as a **dollar-for-dollar credit** against your federal income taxes. On a $24,000 system, that's a **$7,200** reduction in what you owe the IRS.

It's a *credit*, not a deduction — meaning it reduces your tax bill directly, not just your taxable income.

## What costs qualify

The 30% applies to nearly everything on your invoice:

- Solar panels and inverters
- **Battery storage** (3 kWh or larger)
- Racking, wiring and electrical work
- Labor, permitting and inspection costs
- Sales tax on eligible equipment

## Who qualifies

To claim the credit you must:

- **Own** the system (cash purchase or solar loan — not a lease or PPA)
- Have it installed at a U.S. residence you own
- Have enough **federal tax liability** to absorb the credit

## What if my tax bill is too small?

If your credit is larger than what you owe in a single year, the unused portion **rolls forward** to future tax years. You don't lose it — you simply claim the remainder next year.

## How to claim it

1. Keep your final paid invoice from your installer.
2. File **IRS Form 5695** with your federal return for the year the system was placed in service.
3. Carry forward any unused credit to the following year.

## Stack it with state and utility incentives

The federal credit is just the foundation. Depending on where you live, you may also qualify for:

- **State tax credits** (e.g. Arizona's 25% credit)
- **Utility rebates** and performance payments
- **Property- and sales-tax exemptions**

Combined, these can push your effective cost down dramatically.
