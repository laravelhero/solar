Solar panels are only half of the energy-independence story. Panels make power during the day; a **battery** lets you use that power any time — including at night and during outages. So when is storage worth it?

## What a battery actually does

A home battery banks the surplus energy your panels produce during the day and releases it when you need it. That unlocks three benefits:

1. **Backup power** when the grid goes down
2. **Peak-rate avoidance** by using stored power instead of expensive grid power
3. **Higher self-consumption** of your own solar instead of exporting it cheaply

## The outage myth

Here's a surprise for many new solar owners: **standard grid-tied solar shuts off during a blackout.** It's a safety requirement that protects utility crews working on the lines.

That means without a battery, a power outage leaves you in the dark *even with a roof full of panels*. A battery is what keeps your lights, fridge and medical equipment running.

## You probably want a battery if…

- You live somewhere with **frequent outages** — hurricanes, wildfires, ice storms or an unreliable grid
- Your utility uses **time-of-use rates** with expensive evening peaks
- You're on **NEM 3.0** in California, where exporting power is worth far less than using it yourself
- You rely on **medical equipment** or run a home business
- You want genuine **energy independence**

## A battery may be optional if…

- Your grid is highly reliable
- Your utility offers strong **1:1 net metering**, which already credits exported power at full value
- Your budget is tight and your main goal is simply lowering your bill

## How much backup do you really need?

Backup falls into two camps:

- **Partial / essentials backup** — keeps critical loads (fridge, internet, a few outlets, lights) running for a long time on a smaller battery
- **Whole-home backup** — runs everything, including AC and well pumps, usually requiring multiple stacked batteries

Most families start with essentials backup and add capacity later — modern systems are designed to stack.

## The incentive angle

Batteries paired with solar qualify for the same **30% federal tax credit**, and many states layer on storage-specific rebates (California's SGIP, for example). That meaningfully softens the cost.

## The verdict

If resilience, outage protection or time-of-use savings matter to you, a battery turns solar from a daytime discount into true 24/7 energy security. If you only care about shaving your bill and your grid is rock-solid, you can always add storage later.

Not sure which camp you're in? A quick assessment of your utility rate and outage history will make the answer obvious.
