Solar panels have no moving parts, so they're famously low-maintenance — but they're not *no*-maintenance. The single biggest performance thief is something completely invisible from the ground: **dirt**.

## Why clean panels matter

Solar cells generate electricity from sunlight. Anything between the sun and the cell — dust, pollen, bird droppings, ash, agricultural residue — blocks that light and cuts production. Studies consistently show soiling losses of **15–25%**, and in dusty or agricultural environments it can be worse.

That's real money. A 20% production loss on a system that should save you $2,400 a year is **$480 left on the roof** annually.

## How often should you clean?

It depends heavily on your environment:

- **Mild, rainy climates:** once a year is often enough — rain does some of the work.
- **Dusty or arid regions** (Arizona, Nevada): **twice a year** or more.
- **Agricultural areas:** quarterly, due to dust, pollen and crop residue.
- **Near trees or heavy bird activity:** more frequent spot-cleaning of droppings, which are acidic and can etch glass.

A good rule of thumb: if you can *see* a film, haze or droppings on the panels, you're losing production.

## Why rain isn't enough

Rain rinses loose dust but leaves behind a "tide line" of residue — much like a car left out in a storm. Pollen, grime and droppings bond to the glass and need active cleaning to remove fully.

## The case against DIY

It's tempting to grab a hose, but consider:

- **Rooftop falls** are among the most common serious home-injury causes.
- **Hard tap water** leaves mineral spots that themselves reduce output.
- **Abrasive brushes or pressure washers** can scratch the anti-reflective coating or void your warranty.

Professional cleaning uses **deionized (spot-free) water** and **soft, panel-safe brushes**, plus fall-protection equipment and the know-how to avoid damage.

## Cleaning is also an inspection

A professional cleaning visit doubles as a quick health check — a chance to spot hot spots, loose connections, pest damage or cracked panels before they become expensive failures.

## Set it and forget it

The easiest approach is a **scheduled maintenance plan** that cleans and inspects your system seasonally, so you never have to think about it — and never quietly lose a fifth of your production again.
