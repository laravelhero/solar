The combination of solar and an electric vehicle is a financial home run: you generate your own electricity, then use it to replace gasoline entirely. Done right, your daily commute runs on free sunshine.

## Why solar + EV is the perfect pair

Gasoline is a recurring cost you can never escape — until you switch to an EV powered by your own panels. Once your solar covers your driving load, your "fuel" cost drops effectively to zero, locked in for the 25-year life of your system.

## How it works

1. **Your panels** generate electricity during the day.
2. A **Level-2 home charger** (240V) delivers a full charge overnight or a fast top-up during the day.
3. **Smart scheduling** prioritizes charging during your sunniest, cheapest hours.
4. An optional **battery** lets you charge from stored solar in the evening.

## Sizing your system for an EV

The average EV driver adds roughly **2 to 4 kW** of solar to cover typical driving:

- A car driven ~12,000 miles/year uses about **3,500 kWh** annually
- That's roughly **8–10 additional panels** beyond your home's normal needs

The key is sizing your array for your *combined* household-plus-vehicle load — something we model precisely during design.

## Level-1 vs. Level-2 charging

- **Level 1** (standard wall outlet) adds ~3–5 miles of range per hour — fine for plug-in hybrids, too slow for most EVs.
- **Level 2** (240V) adds ~25–40 miles per hour — a full overnight charge, every night. This is what we recommend and install.

## The smart-charging advantage

Modern chargers integrate with your solar and utility rates to:

- Charge during **peak solar production**
- Avoid expensive **time-of-use peak windows**
- Pause and resume based on **real-time** household demand

## Don't forget the incentives

Both the solar and, in many cases, the EV charging equipment can qualify for federal and local incentives. The **30% federal tax credit** applies to the solar portion, and many utilities offer rebates on Level-2 chargers and special EV charging rates.

## The bottom line

Solar plus EV charging is the closest thing to free transportation energy available today. If you drive an EV — or plan to — designing your solar system with that load in mind from the start is the smartest path to driving on sunshine.
